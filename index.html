History of Pi

The symbol π was first used to represent the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter by Welsh mathematician William Jones in 1706. The Greek letter was chosen because it is the first letter of the Greek word "perimeter".

Calculation

Pi is an irrational number, meaning it cannot be expressed as a simple fraction and its decimal representation never ends or settles into a permanently repeating pattern. Modern computers have calculated Pi to trillions of digits.

Applications

Pi appears in many formulas in mathematics and physics, including areas of circles, volumes of spheres, and in wave equations. It's essential in engineering, construction, and signal processing.
